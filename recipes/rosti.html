Classic Swiss Rösti

Home

Rösti is the ultimate Swiss comfort food. Originally a breakfast dish for farmers in the canton of Bern, it is now enjoyed nationwide as a versatile main course or a side dish. This crispy, golden potato pancake relies on simplicity and patience to achieve the perfect texture.

The secret to a great Rösti lies in the choice of potato and the frying process. While some prefer using boiled potatoes from the day before, this recipe uses raw potatoes for a slightly firmer bite and a beautiful crust.

Ingredients

The Base:

  • Starchy potatoes (e.g., Russet or Yukon Gold)
  • Clarified butter (Ghee) or Butter
  • Fine sea salt

Optional Add-ins:

  • Smoked bacon bits (Speck)
  • Diced onions
  • Gruyère cheese (for topping)
  • Fresh rosemary

Steps

  1. Prepare the potatoes: Peel the potatoes and grate them using the coarse side of a box grater. If the potatoes are very wet, gently squeeze out the excess moisture with a clean kitchen towel.
  2. Season: In a bowl, toss the shredded potatoes with a generous pinch of salt. If using onions or bacon, mix them in now.
  3. Start frying: Heat the clarified butter in a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Add the potatoes, spreading them out evenly. Lightly press them down with a spatula to form a cake.
  4. Cook and Shape: Cook for about 10-12 minutes without stirring. Occasionally push the edges in to maintain a neat circular shape. The bottom should be deep golden brown and crispy.
  5. The Flip: Place a large flat plate over the skillet. Carefully flip the skillet so the Rösti lands on the plate. Add a bit more butter to the pan, then slide the Rösti back in to brown the other side for another 10 minutes.
