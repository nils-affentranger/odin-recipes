Älplermagronen (Alpine Macaroni)

Home

Known as the "Alpine Macaroni," this dish was created for mountain herders who needed hearty, non-perishable ingredients like dried pasta, potatoes, and cheese. It is traditionally served with a side of applesauce to cut through the richness of the cream and cheese.

The combination of pasta and potatoes might seem unusual, but it creates a wonderfully filling texture that is quintessential to Swiss mountain culture.

Ingredients

The Main Dish:

  • Short pasta (Penne or Macaroni)
  • Firm-boiling potatoes
  • Heavy cream
  • Shredded Gruyère or Appenzeller cheese
  • Vegetable broth

The Garnish:

  • Onions (thinly sliced)
  • Butter
  • Applesauce (served on the side)
  • Crispy fried onions

Steps

  1. Boil potatoes and pasta: Peel and cube the potatoes. Bring the vegetable broth to a boil in a large pot. Add the potatoes and cook for 5 minutes, then add the pasta to the same pot and cook until both are tender.
  2. Drain and Cream: Drain most of the liquid, leaving just a splash. Pour in the heavy cream and bring to a simmer over low heat.
  3. Add Cheese: Stir in the shredded cheese gradually. Mix gently until the cheese has melted into a smooth, thick sauce that coats the pasta and potatoes. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Caramelize Onions: While the pasta cooks, melt butter in a separate pan. Sauté the sliced onions over medium heat until they are dark brown and caramelized.
  5. Serve: Plate the macaroni and potato mixture, top with the caramelized onions, and serve immediately with a generous bowl of chilled applesauce on the side.
